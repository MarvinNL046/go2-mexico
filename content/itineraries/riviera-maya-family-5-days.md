## Overview

The [Riviera Maya](/destinations/riviera-maya/) is one of Mexico's best destinations for families, with warm Caribbean water, accessible eco-parks, gentle cenotes for swimming, and ruins that ignite kids' imaginations. This 5-day itinerary balances activity with downtime, suitable for families with children of all ages.

## Day 1: Arrive and Beach Day

- Morning: Arrive at Cancun airport, transfer to [Playa del Carmen](/cities/playa-del-carmen/) (1 hour)
- Afternoon: Beach time at Playacar or Mamitas beach -- shallow water, soft sand
- Evening: Stroll Fifth Avenue, dinner at a family-friendly restaurant

## Day 2: Xcaret Eco-Park

- Full day: Xcaret eco-archaeological park -- underground rivers for floating, snorkeling inlet, butterfly pavilion, aviary, sea turtle conservation area, and children's activities
- Evening: Stay for the evening Mexico Espectacular show -- a cultural performance with traditional music and dance that kids enjoy. Dinner included with some packages.

## Day 3: Tulum Ruins and Cenotes

- Early morning: Visit [Tulum Ruins](/destinations/tulum-ruins/) at 8:00 AM opening -- the clifftop ruins are dramatic and the site is manageable for kids (1-1.5 hours)
- Mid-morning: Swim at the beach below the ruins
- Late morning: [Gran Cenote](/destinations/cenotes-yucatan/) -- snorkeling in shallow, crystal-clear water perfect for families. Turtles are often visible.
- Afternoon: Cenote Azul near Playa del Carmen -- a large, open cenote with shallow areas for younger children
- Evening: Relaxed dinner back in Playa del Carmen

## Day 4: Xel-Ha or Cozumel

- **Option A: Xel-Ha** -- An all-inclusive natural aquarium park. Kids can snorkel in calm, shallow water full of fish. Unlimited food, drinks, and activities. Inner tubes, kayaks, and a cliff jump area for older kids and adults.
- **Option B: [Cozumel](/destinations/cozumel/)** -- Ferry from Playa del Carmen (45 minutes). Snorkeling at Chankanaab Park, explore by golf cart, visit El Cielo sandbar by boat.
- Evening: Pizza or tacos for tired but happy kids

## Day 5: Adventure Park or Beach, Departure

- Morning: Choose based on energy levels --
  - [Zip-lining](/experiences/zip-lining-riviera-maya/) at a family-friendly course (most have age/weight minimums)
  - Final beach morning with paddleboarding or kayaking
  - Shopping for souvenirs along Fifth Avenue
- Afternoon: Transfer to Cancun airport for departure

## Practical Information

- **Budget estimate** -- $120-200 USD per day for a family of four (mid-range, including park tickets)
- **Park tickets** -- Xcaret and Xel-Ha are expensive (2,000-4,000 MXN per adult) but all-inclusive. Book online in advance for discounts of 10-20%.
- **Transport** -- Rent a car for flexibility with kids, or use colectivos for the Playa-Tulum corridor. Car seats are rarely provided by rental companies -- consider bringing your own for young children.
- **Sun safety** -- Reef-safe sunscreen is required at cenotes and eco-parks. The tropical sun is strong -- reapply frequently and keep kids hydrated.
- **Best time** -- November to April for dry weather and calm seas. Avoid Semana Santa when everything is crowded and expensive.
