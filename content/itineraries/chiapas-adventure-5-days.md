## Overview

Chiapas is one of Mexico's most rewarding states for adventurous travelers. This 5-day itinerary covers the major highlights -- from the highland colonial charm of [San Cristobal de las Casas](/destinations/san-cristobal-de-las-casas/) to the jungle-draped ruins of [Palenque](/destinations/palenque/) and the dramatic [Sumidero Canyon](/destinations/sumidero-canyon/).

## Day 1: Arrive in San Cristobal de las Casas

- Arrive via Tuxtla Gutierrez airport (TGZ), 1 hour transfer to San Cristobal
- Afternoon: Walk the Andador Eclesiastico pedestrian street, visit Santo Domingo church and the textile cooperative
- Evening: Explore the amber market, warm up with Chiapan hot chocolate, dinner of tamales de chipilin

## Day 2: Indigenous Communities and Markets

- Morning: Guided visit to San Juan Chamula -- the remarkable church where Mayan and Catholic traditions merge (photography forbidden inside; follow all guidelines)
- Late morning: Visit Zinacantan for textile demonstrations and floral embroidery
- Lunch: Traditional meal in Zinacantan with a local family (arranged through tour)
- Afternoon: Back in San Cristobal -- explore Na Bolom museum, browse artisan shops
- Evening: Dinner at a local restaurant, try posh (traditional sugar cane spirit) and Chiapan coffee

## Day 3: Sumidero Canyon and Transfer

- Early morning: Drive to Chiapa de Corzo (1 hour)
- Morning: [Sumidero Canyon](/destinations/sumidero-canyon/) boat tour through the dramatic canyon -- crocodiles, monkeys, and the Christmas Tree waterfall
- Lunch: In Chiapa de Corzo -- explore the town plaza and lacquerware artisans
- Afternoon: Transfer toward Palenque (5 hours by road, or return to Tuxtla for a short flight)
- Evening: Arrive in Palenque town, dinner at a local restaurant

## Day 4: Palenque Ruins and Waterfalls

- Early morning: [Palenque archaeological site](/destinations/palenque/) -- arrive at 8:00 AM opening for the best experience. Temple of the Inscriptions, the Palace, the Temple of the Cross group.
- Late morning: Visit the site museum for Pakal's tomb replica and artifact context
- Afternoon: Drive to Agua Azul waterfalls (about 1.5 hours) -- swim in the turquoise cascading pools. Or visit Misol-Ha waterfall (closer, 30 minutes) with its path behind the waterfall curtain.
- Evening: Return to Palenque town for dinner

## Day 5: Return and Departure

- Morning: Optional visit to Bonampak (famous for Mayan murals) if time permits, or a relaxed morning exploring Palenque town
- Transfer to Villahermosa airport (2 hours) or Palenque airport for departure
- Alternative: Some travelers continue to the Yucatan from Palenque -- buses run to Campeche and Merida

## Practical Information

- **Budget estimate** -- $50-100 USD per day for mid-range travel
- **Transport** -- Rent a car for flexibility, or use ADO/OCC buses and organized tours between destinations. The San Cristobal to Palenque road is scenic but long and winding.
- **Altitude** -- San Cristobal is at 2,200 meters. Pack warm layers for cool mornings and evenings.
- **Best time** -- November to April for dry weather. Agua Azul is most impressive during or just after the rainy season when water flow is high.
- **Respect** -- Chiapas has a complex political and cultural landscape. Treat indigenous communities with respect and always ask before photographing people.
