## Overview

This 5-day itinerary covers the highlights of the [Yucatan Peninsula](/regions/yucatan-peninsula/), from Caribbean beaches and ancient Mayan ruins to mystical cenotes. Starting in Cancun and moving south along the Riviera Maya, it balances adventure with relaxation.

## Day 1: Cancun Arrival and Isla Mujeres

- Morning: Arrive at Cancun airport, transfer to hotel
- Late morning: Ferry to [Isla Mujeres](/destinations/isla-mujeres/) from Puerto Juarez (20 minutes)
- Afternoon: Rent a golf cart and explore the island. Swim at Playa Norte, visit Punta Sur.
- Evening: Seafood dinner on the island, return by ferry to Cancun

## Day 2: Playa del Carmen and Cenotes

- Morning: Transfer to [Playa del Carmen](/cities/playa-del-carmen/) (1 hour south). Drop bags at hotel.
- Late morning: [Cenote swimming](/experiences/cenote-swimming/) -- visit Cenote Azul and Jardin del Eden
- Afternoon: Stroll Fifth Avenue, lunch at a seafood restaurant
- Evening: Explore Playa's restaurant and bar scene

## Day 3: Tulum Ruins and Beach

- Morning: Early visit to [Tulum Ruins](/destinations/tulum-ruins/) (arrive at 8:00 AM opening to beat crowds)
- Mid-morning: Swim at the beach below the ruins
- Late morning: [Gran Cenote](/destinations/cenotes-yucatan/) for snorkeling in crystal-clear cave water
- Afternoon: Explore [Tulum](/cities/tulum/) beach zone, lunch at a beachfront restaurant
- Evening: Return to Playa del Carmen or stay in Tulum

## Day 4: Chichen Itza and Valladolid

- Early morning: Drive or take a tour to [Chichen Itza](/destinations/chichen-itza/) (2.5 hours from Playa)
- Morning: Explore the archaeological site with a guide -- El Castillo, the Ball Court, the Observatory
- Lunch: In Valladolid, a charming colonial city 40 minutes from the ruins
- Afternoon: Swim at Cenote Suytun or Cenote Zaci in Valladolid
- Evening: Dinner in Valladolid's plaza, overnight in Valladolid or return to base

## Day 5: Beach Day and Departure

- Morning: Final beach time -- choose between Cancun Hotel Zone, Playa del Carmen, or a quieter stretch
- Optional: Snorkeling trip, spa morning, or shopping for souvenirs
- Afternoon: Transfer to Cancun airport for departure

## Practical Information

- **Budget estimate** -- $70-130 USD per day for mid-range travel
- **Transport** -- Rent a car for maximum flexibility, or use ADO buses and colectivos between towns
- **Best time** -- November to April for dry weather. Avoid Semana Santa (Easter week) when prices spike and beaches are packed.
- **Booking tip** -- Reserve Chichen Itza tours or rental car in advance during peak season
